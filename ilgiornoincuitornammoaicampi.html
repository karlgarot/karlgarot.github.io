about.

Il Giorno in cui Tornammo ai Campi (The Day We Returned to the Fields) is an installation that takes the form of a multimedia landscape, in which the means of technical reading of the territory leave their usual sphere of application to become a sensitive monograph of places, those of the disused industrial areas bordering the Fondovalle del Sinni, in Basilicata. As visitors follow the exhibition route, cartography, photogrammetry and photography on technical paper dictate the slow approach towards the microscopic datum, a sensitive representation of those places. The video installation Zone Morte (Dead Zones) is a virtual journey inside the database of the Basilicata Geoportal, which returns, by subtraction, the map of industrial voids. Wind speed, temperature, humidity, barometric pressure, rain, air quality, sound, movement, are some of the data collected in real time by Resìdua, a self-built device using electronic material found in those places, to monitor their biological transformations. The resulting data sculpting is a living organism responsive to visitor contact.







"Today those places, a visual friction of the landscape, stand on both sides of the Sinni Valley State Road 653, shaping its horizon, diverting the gaze of the passer-by driving along it. From Lauria to Policoloro, passing through the villages of Latronico, Episcopia, Fardella, Chiaromonte and Francavilla, and then on to Senise, Colobraro, Noepoli, Valsinni and Tursi, the disused industrial areas embody the identity of these communities, a shared fragment of a collective conscience that unites us under a common yesterday"
